Commerce Reimagined Intelligently

Turning eCommerce Data into Actionable Business Strategy:
Exploring customer behaviour and sales trends.

Goal

The goal of this project is to transform raw eCommerce data into actionable business intelligence that supports strategic decision-making across marketing, sales, customer retention, and operations. By leveraging the synthetic yet richly layered "TheLook" dataset from Google BigQuery. This solution aims to:

  • Uncover patterns in customer purchase behavior to distinguish between one-time and repeat buyers and identify opportunities to increase customer lifetime value.
  • Track and interpret sales performance trends across time, geography, and product categories to enable data-driven forecasting and seasonal strategy planning.
  • Measure and evaluate marketing channel effectiveness to help optimize budget allocation and improve return on advertising spend.
  • Monitor operational bottlenecks, including order fulfillment statuses and delivery delays, to improve customer satisfaction and streamline logistics.
  • Enable dynamic, drillable reporting to support both high-level executive overviews and in-depth analyst exploration of KPIs across multiple filters such as time, location, product brand/category, and traffic source.
This dashboard empowers stakeholders with the insights needed to make confident, evidence-based business decisions that enhance revenue growth, improve customer loyalty, and drive operational excellence.

Tools & Skills Used:

  • Excel: Data exploration and initial cleansing.
  • SQL Server: Data hosting and structured querying.
  • SSIS (SQL Server Integration Services): ETL pipeline for importing and transforming multi-table datasets.
  • Power Query: Data cleaning, shaping, and pre-aggregation.
  • Power BI Reporting: Developed intuitive and filterable visuals to uncover business-critical insights.
  • Data Modeling & Schema Design: Built relationships between 7 normalized tables using star schema principles.
  • DAX: Created dynamic KPIs, calculated fields, measures, and user behavior insights.
  • ETL Process Design: Extracted and transformed high-volume data using SSIS into SQL Server.
  • Interpreted data patterns into clear strategic actions
  • Analytical thinking and Storytelling with Data

Project Description

This project focuses on deriving actionable business intelligence from TheLook eCommerce dataset hosted on Google BigQuery. Using a combination of SSIS, SQL Server, and Power BI, I developed a dynamic analytics solution to uncover key patterns in customer behavior, sales performance, marketing effectiveness, and fulfillment efficiency. Here are what I included:

Key Metrics at a glance:

  • Strategic KPIs for transaction performance.
  • Customer purchase behaviour.
  • Customer behavioral analysis.

Visuals that tell a story:

  • Sales Insight: Monthly trends via dynamic line chart using the selected parameter.
  • Regional Analysis: City & State insights through interactive bar & map visuals.
  • Marketing Channel Performance: Pie chart segmented by traffic source (Email, Facebook, YouTube, etc.).
  • Order Fulfillment Status: Donut chart of Shipped, Complete, Cancelled, Returned, and Processing orders.
  • Top Performing Product Categories: Bar chart with dynamic measure selector or filters

Drilled Dataset View:

  • Detailed data records behind each visual
  • Supports exploration and validation of KPIs

Dataset Overview (7 Tables): The dataset consist of events table (web activity) with 2,425,956 rows, distribution_centers table(10 rows), inventory_items (489,368 rows), order_items(181,321 rows), products (29,120 rows), users (100,000 rows), and orders table (125,042 rows)

Key findings

  • Identified that 30,056 customers made multiple purchases, indicating the potential for targeted loyalty programs.
  • Customer repeat rate averaged 38%, highlighting strong brand engagement within key segments.
  • High-value customers showed 1.56 purchase frequency and a lifetime value of $33.3, essential for retention strategy
  • Total sales revenue reached $2,668,483 from 343,701 completed orders (Excluding cancelled and orders in process ).
  • Sales exhibited noticeable fluctuations, with peak performance observed from December through May, revealing opportunities for seasonal marketing alignment.
  • Countries such as China and the United States emerged as top-performing regions by sales.
  • Organic traffic drove 5% of overall visits, while email marketing contributed 45%, indicating strong engagement from existing customers and effective newsletter campaigns.
  • Sweaters and Suits & Sport Coats consistently generate the highest revenue and units sold.
