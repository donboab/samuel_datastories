Coffee Vending Insight

Dashboard Tracking Sales Trends, Customer Preferences & Operational Health

Goal

Sometimes around this year, I was given a clear goal to turn raw sales data from a coffee vending machine into something meaningful that could help people make better business decisions. The vending machine had been in use since March 2024, and every single transaction including the date, time, coffee type, how it was paid for, and how much money came in had been recorded. But until now, that information was just sitting in a spreadsheet, hard to read and even harder to act on.

So, I was asked to build a dashboard that is not just something that looked nice, but something that could genuinely answer questions like:

  • What coffee do people love the most?
  • When do we sell the most coffee during the day or week?
  • Are people using cards more than cash now?
  • How is our revenue trending month by month?

Tools & Skills Used:

  • Power BI: Built the entire dashboard using Power BI, including interactive charts, slicers, and charts.
  • DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Created custom measures for KPI and other calculated values
  • Data cleaning and transformation
  • Power Query
  • Data visualization and dashboard design
  • Analytical thinking and Storytelling with Data

Project Description

To bring clarity to the data, I built an interactive dashboard in Power BI, designed for both everyday users and business decision-makers. It combines key numbers, visual trends, and patterns in a way that’s easy to explore. Here’s what I included:

Key Metrics at a glance:

  • Total revenue earned so far.
  • The average amount we make each day.
  • The coffee that brings in the most money.

Visuals that tell a story:

  • A monthly sales trend line to show growth (or dips) over time.
  • A bar chart of the top 5 coffee types based on revenue.
  • A heatmap that shows exactly what time of day and what day of the week people buy the most coffee.
  • A pie chart breaking down sales by card and cash.
  • A line chart showing how payment preferences have changed over time

Key findings

  • Revenue Performance & Sales Patterns: The vending machine generated a total revenue of £115,432 over the analysis period. A consistent upward trend in monthly sales was observed, with peaks occurring toward the end of the year indicating seasonal and behavioral patterns in customer purchases.
  • Top Sellers Identified: The most purchased coffee type was Latte, accounting for £27,866 of total sales. The top 5 coffee types collectively drove a significant portion of revenue, suggesting that streamlining inventory around these high performers could enhance profitability and reduce waste.
  • Operational Timing for Peak Hours and Days: The heatmap analysis revealed that peak vending activity occurred during 8 a.m. and 11 a.m., especially on Mondays, Tuesdays and Fridays, providing insights for restocking schedules and potential promotional timing.
  • Payment Method Trends:
    • Cash transactions made up 2.76% of total sales, while card payments accounted for 97.24%. Card payments showed a noticeable acceptance, reflecting customer payment preferences.
